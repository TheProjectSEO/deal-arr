12-month strategy · Foundation → Engine → AEO → Moat

The sequence matters. More than the tactics.

Most B2B SaaS SEO strategies list 40 tactics and pick whichever one's easy first. Ours runs in strict dependency order — because publishing 50 BOFU pages before fixing render blocks produces zero ranking, and booking Nina's podcast tour before the site can be cited in shownotes produces dead links. Sequence first, volume second.

The six pillars

Six pillars, sequenced — each feeds the next

These are the dependency chain, not a menu. Pillar 1 unblocks Pillar 2. Pillar 2 feeds Pillar 3. And so on.

Pillar 01 · Weeks 1-6

Technical foundation

Platform-layer rendering remediation. Nothing else works until Google, Bing, and the LLM crawlers can actually see the site. We architect the architectural decision with your CTO (three viable paths, each with specific tradeoffs on engineering effort, maintenance, AEO, and 18-month flexibility); your engineering team executes. Then schema site-wide, GSC + GA4 + Bing Webmaster + attribution plumbing, Core Web Vitals green. The specific remediation framework is decided post-signing — publishing generic advice here would push you toward the cheapest option, which is almost always wrong for a VC-funded SaaS.

Pillar 02 · Months 2-4

Topical architecture + foundation pages

5 L1 pillar pages (RevOps Platform, SaaS Metrics, Deal Management, CS + Churn, Board Reporting). 25 L2 sub-topic pages (ARR, MRR, NRR, churn, commissions, revenue recognition, financial modeling, etc.). Internal link architecture. This becomes the topical authority spine every BOFU page hangs off. Source: Ahrefs — Content Hubs; Moz — B2B Pillar Pages.

Pillar 03 · Months 3-7

BOFU programmatic

40 bottom-of-funnel pages: "DealARR vs [competitor]" × 10, "[competitor] alternative" × 10, "RevOps for [segment]" × 10, use-case pages × 10. These rank fast (some at KD 0) and convert directly — self-serve signup is inline. Pipedrive increased organic sign-ups 33% from this exact motion. Source: Moz — Pipedrive BOFU Case Study; Moz — Customer Journey Keywords.

Pillar 04 · Months 2-12 (parallel)

AEO + LLM citation layer

60+ FAQ-rich capsule pages designed to be cited by ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini. Entity clarity (DealARR specifically defined, not "another AI RevOps tool"). Schema-marked Q&A blocks. Targets positions 21+ because that's where 90% of LLM citations come from. Source: Backlinko — LLM Visibility; Moz — Traditional SEO Becoming Obsolete.

Pillar 05 · Months 3-12 (parallel)

Listicle placement engine

Every RevOps SERP in top 10 is a listicle (ORM, Zapier, Gartner, G2, Forecastio). Outreach queue of 30 publications, 10 podcast hosts, 5 analyst firms. Goal: DealARR in 6-10 listicles by Month 10. Each listicle ≈ 200-800 qualified monthly visits — every visitor is in-market, every placement is a direct path to a self-serve signup. Source: Moz — B2B content marketing.

Pillar 06 · Months 2-12 (Nina-led)

Founder-led category authority

Nina's LinkedIn (3 posts/wk), 20+ podcast guest appearances over 12 months, quarterly "State of RevOps for Series A-C SaaS" data report, 5-8 guest posts on SaaStr / First Round / Women in Finance. Builds the entity signal LLMs mine. Category design play — make "AI-native RevOps" == "Nina Alag Suri + DealARR." Source: Backlinko — LLM Seeding; Moz — Entity Clarity for LLMs.

Why this sequence

The dependency chain, stated explicitly

If any of these assumptions breaks, the sequence changes. Stating them upfront so we can re-plan fast if reality pushes back.

1. Platform fix gates everything

If the rendering remediation ships in Week 2-4, Gate 1 is achievable. If it slips to Month 3, Gate 1 slips to Month 5-6. Our #1 Week 1 priority is working with your CTO to pick the right architectural path — three viable options, each with specific tradeoffs we'll map against your stack, team capacity, and runway.

2. Pillar pages unlock BOFU velocity

BOFU pages (vs-competitor, alternatives) convert only when they internally link to credible topical pillar content. Shipping them without the L1/L2 foundation leaves them orphaned and under-ranked. That's why Pillar 2 comes before Pillar 3.

3. AEO is a parallel track, not a sequential one

Most agencies treat AEO as "Phase 3." Wrong. AEO capsules, entity clarity, and brand mentions compound slowly — we start them in Month 2 so the moat is built by Month 10. Starting in Month 9 means we're still figuring out what works at Month 14.

4. Nina's LinkedIn + podcast is not "optional marketing"

Nina has category authority (X0PA, Nastrac, Enterprise Asia recognition, APAC top-100). LLMs mine LinkedIn, podcasts, Medium, and earned coverage to decide "who's the authority on AI-native RevOps?" Founder-led content is the single highest-ROI lever. If Nina doesn't have 45 min/wk for this, Gate 3 category ownership doesn't happen.

5. Listicle outreach takes 8-16 weeks per placement

We can't manufacture listicle speed. Outreach in Month 3 → first placement Month 5-7 (if the listicle owner updates quarterly). That's why Gate 2 "3+ listicles" is realistic and Gate 3 "6+ listicles" is the compound result, not a fast-track.

6. PLG motion = attribution compounds

DealARR's no-demo, no-sales-call model means the attribution loop closes in ~10 minutes from click to signup. This is RARE and incredibly valuable. Every page we ship can drive measurable pipeline within days of ranking — not months after a sales-led cycle. That's why gate metrics focus on signups / paid customers, not just rankings.
