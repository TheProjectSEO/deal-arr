The setup

What dealarr.com actually looks like to Google right now

Before planning what to build, we fetched dealarr.com with Googlebot's user-agent to see what the crawler actually receives. The result is the single most important finding on this page.

Technical diagnosis · 2026-04-20

dealarr.com is a fully client-side rendered SPA. The raw HTML body contains exactly two indexable strings: "DealARR" and "Loading…". Everything else — every feature, every heading, every module description, every schema snippet — exists only after JavaScript hydrates in a browser. Googlebot can eventually render JS and Google claims it does so within ~5 seconds (per Ahrefs, citing Martin Splitt at Chrome Dev Summit), but even Ahrefs concludes "is client-side rendering and leaving Googlebot to render content the right strategy? The answer is most likely still no." Bing, Yandex, Baidu and most social bots have limited JS support, which is why OG/Twitter tags don't preview on most platforms.

The shape of the work

Five strategic wedges we'd press — ordered by asymmetry

These aren't tactics. They're the five places DealARR can beat well-funded incumbents because the category hasn't yet priced them in. Each one details out into content, pages, and plays across the 12-month arc.

Wedge 01

AEO before the incumbents price it in

"Almost 90% of ChatGPT's citations come from search results ranking in positions 21+ — not the top 5." — Backlinko, LLM Visibility

B2B SaaS buyers already research in ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini. DealARR can win LLM citation without fighting for Google #1 — via schema, entity clarity, FAQ capsules, and being mentioned in the places LLMs mine. AEO is not a 2027 problem. It's a this-quarter problem.

Wedge 02

Listicle insertion, not listicle ownership

Every top-10 SERP result for "RevOps tools for B2B SaaS" is a listicle — ORM Technologies, Zapier, Gartner, G2, Forecastio, AutoRFP. None mention DealARR yet. Outreach + relationship = 6-10 listicle placements in Months 3-6. Each is worth more than ranking #15 ourselves, because every listicle visitor is in-market. BOFU-first — every external placement is a direct path to a self-serve signup.

Wedge 03

Competitor-displacement at KD 0

"Gong alternative" (vol 150, KD 0). "ChartMogul alternative" (vol 100). "Mosaic alternative" (vol 30). "Maxio alternative" (vol 10). Combined search volume ~300/mo of pure commercial-intent traffic where a decent DealARR page ranks in weeks. Not months. Weeks.

Wedge 04

Category design, not category competition

The SERP for "AI RevOps platform" is chaotic — Inventive, Momentum, Glean, Fullcast all point at different problems. No one owns "AI-Native RevOps for Series A-C SaaS." With Nina's existing category authority (X0PA, Nastrac, Enterprise Asia award, APAC top-100), DealARR can claim this phrase via founder-led LinkedIn, 20+ podcast guest spots, and a "State of RevOps 2026" data report. Play Bigger category design, SEO-amplified.

Wedge 05

PLG funnel = SEO compounds directly

No demos. No sales calls. 7-day free trial. Every SEO-attributed visit can convert in minutes. This is the rarest unit economics structure in B2B SaaS SEO — most RevOps competitors have 30-60 day sales cycles where organic attribution goes fuzzy. For DealARR, organic visit → signup → activation loop closes in under 10 minutes. The attribution math is clean.
