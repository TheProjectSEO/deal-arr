Part 1 · The scope, phased

Three phases, twelve months, one compounding engine

Every phase has a specific gate. Every gate has a floor (what unlocks the next tier) and a stretch (what accelerates us ahead of plan). Monthly outputs compound — we don't reset deliverables each month.

Phase 1 · Months 1-4

Foundation · make the site indexable

  • SSR or prerender deployment (Prerender.io or Next.js migration — we advise, you ship)
  • Schema markup site-wide: Organization, SoftwareApplication, Product, FAQPage, Article, Person, BreadcrumbList
  • GSC + GA4 + Ahrefs + Bing Webmaster wired, attribution live
  • 25 indexable pages shipped — 5 L1 pillars, 15 L2 sub-topics, 5 BOFU
  • Technical audit → fix queue → ship + verify
  • Competitor comparison stack starts (DealARR vs ChartMogul / Maxio / Mosaic)
  • LinkedIn founder content cadence with Nina (3 posts/wk)
  • First 3 listicle outreach sequences opened
Phase 2 · Months 5-7

Content engine · BOFU programmatic

  • 50 additional indexable pages shipped (cumulative 75)
  • All competitor-displacement pages live: "gong alternative," "chartmogul alternative," "mosaic alternative," "maxio alternative," "clari alternative"
  • Use-case cluster built: RevOps for Series A / B / C; RevOps for FinTech / MarTech / HRTech / HealthTech
  • AEO capsule stack: 30 FAQ-rich pages targeting LLM citation
  • 3+ listicle placements secured (ORM, Forecastio, AutoRFP, Zapier)
  • First podcast bookings for Nina (3-5 pods in pipeline)
  • First research artifact: "State of RevOps for Series A-C SaaS 2026" data pull
Phase 3 · Months 8-12

AEO + category · compound moat

  • 75 additional pages (cumulative 150)
  • AEO-first content: category definition posts, benchmark data, entity disambiguation
  • Link velocity: 10+ DR 40+ backlinks earned through research content + podcast guesting
  • Category phrase ownership: "AI-native RevOps" surfaces Nina / DealARR in LLM answers
  • G2 + Capterra + Gartner review acceleration playbook
  • Quarterly State of RevOps report — content flywheel
  • Month 12: DealARR appears in 8-10 industry listicles, 10+ AEO citations, compounding MRR attribution from organic
Part 2 · The KPI gates

Three gates. Each has a floor and a stretch. You see exactly what unlocks each tier.

This is the same gate structure we use for SureSpace and every other value-based engagement. Floor = retainer unlocks. Stretch = we fast-track or bonus. Miss the floor, tier doesn't move. Built so nobody eats a silent loss — not you, not us.

Gate 1 · End of Month 4 — Foundation unlock
The site is indexable. Real content is live. Attribution is wired. Early rankings start appearing.
Floor — unlocks tier
  • SSR / prerender deployed — site is indexable by Googlebot, Bing, social bots
  • 25+ indexable pages live (L1 + L2 + first BOFU)
  • Schema site-wide (7 types minimum)
  • 3+ keywords ranking top 20
  • 1st AEO citation in ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini for a target query
  • OR 15+ app signups/mo attributed to organic (PLG attribution via UTM + first-touch)
Stretch — upside cases
  • 35+ signups/mo attributed to organic → validates Phase 2 velocity thesis
  • 1st listicle placement secured (ORM, Forecastio, AutoRFP, Zapier) → pulls Phase 2 gate forward
  • 5+ keywords in top 10 (instead of top 20)
  • 3+ AEO citations across LLMs
Gate 2 · End of Month 7 — Content engine + AEO unlock
Programmatic BOFU is live. Listicles start converting. AEO citations multiply. Category phrase starts showing up in Nina's name.
Floor — unlocks tier
  • 75+ indexable pages (incl. full BOFU stack)
  • 10+ keywords ranking top 10
  • 5+ AEO citations across ChatGPT + Perplexity + Gemini
  • 3+ listicle placements secured
  • 50+ signups/mo attributed to organic OR 10+ MQL/mo from organic (if attribution has gaps)
  • 1+ paid customer/mo from organic (early indicator of Phase 3 payoff)
Stretch — upside cases
  • 100+ signups/mo attributed to organic → pulls Gate 3 forward by 4-6 weeks
  • 3+ paid customers/mo → accelerates to terminal tier
  • 5+ podcast guest appearances scheduled or aired
  • "AI-native RevOps" returning DealARR in 3+ LLM responses
Gate 3 · End of Month 10 — Category ownership + terminal unlock
DealARR is the recurring answer when Series A-C founders research RevOps. LLMs cite consistently. MRR compounds. The engine runs.
Floor — unlocks tier
  • 150+ indexable pages (5 pillars, 30 L2, 40 BOFU, 60+ AEO)
  • 25+ keywords ranking top 10
  • 10+ AEO citations across LLMs
  • 6+ listicle placements across the industry
  • 100+ signups/mo OR 3+ paid customers/mo attributed to organic
  • 10+ DR 40+ earned backlinks
Stretch — upside cases
  • Category phrase ownership — "AI-native RevOps" returns DealARR in 8+ LLM answers
  • 5+ paid customers/mo attributed to organic → MRR compounding
  • MRR from organic ≥ retainer within 2 months of Gate 3
  • "State of RevOps 2026" report picked up in industry media
Part 3 · Deliverables

What you get every month — and what we don't do

B2B SaaS retainers drift into activity theater (monthly reports no one reads, PDFs nobody opens). Our monthly deliverable is narrow and production-grade.

What we ship every month

Production output · not slides

  • 8-12 published pages (content + schema + internal links)
  • Technical fix queue with PRs / specs your dev team can execute in hours, not days
  • LinkedIn content calendar for Nina (3 posts/wk, drafted)
  • Listicle / podcast outreach queue — live tracker, not a PDF
  • AEO citation tracker: what queries are we cited on, trend week over week
  • Rankings dashboard: top 50 target keywords, live
  • Monthly call — 45 min max, tactical, founder-to-founder
What we don't do

The reverse list

  • No monthly "SEO reports" in PDF — dashboards only
  • No content for content's sake (we don't ship a post unless it targets a specific BOFU or AEO outcome)
  • No link building from PBNs, Fiverr, or "guest post networks" — earned + relationship-based only
  • No generic AI-written content — every page passes our anti-AI validator before shipping
  • No lock-in contracts — month-to-month after first 90 days
  • No gatekeeping of working files — Google Drive + GitHub + dashboards all shared, editor access
