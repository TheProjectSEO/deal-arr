Part 1 · The scope, phased

Three phases, twelve months, one compounding engine

Every phase has a specific gate. Every gate has a floor (what unlocks the next tier) and a stretch (what accelerates us ahead of plan). Monthly outputs compound — we don't reset deliverables each month.

Phase 1 · Months 1-4

Foundation · make the site indexable

  • SSR or prerender deployment (Prerender.io or Next.js migration — we advise, you ship)
  • Schema markup site-wide: Organization, SoftwareApplication, Product, FAQPage, Article, Person, BreadcrumbList
  • GSC + GA4 + Ahrefs + Bing Webmaster wired, attribution live
  • 25 indexable pages shipped — 5 L1 pillars, 15 L2 sub-topics, 5 BOFU
  • Technical audit → fix queue → ship + verify
  • Competitor comparison stack starts (DealARR vs ChartMogul / Maxio / Mosaic)
  • LinkedIn founder content cadence with Nina (3 posts/wk)
  • First 3 listicle outreach sequences opened
Phase 2 · Months 5-7

Content engine · the 30 BOFU pages

  • 30 BOFU pages shipped — the full comparison + alternative + use-case stack Series A-C buyers actually search (detailed in next section)
  • Full L2 topical spine — 20+ sub-topic pages anchoring the BOFU pages (cumulative 75)
  • AEO capsule stack: 30 FAQ-rich pages targeting LLM citation
  • 3+ listicle placements secured (ORM, Forecastio, AutoRFP, Zapier)
  • First podcast bookings for Nina (3-5 pods in pipeline)
  • First research artifact: "State of RevOps for Series A-C SaaS 2026" data pull
Phase 3 · Months 8-12

AEO + category · compound moat

  • 75 additional pages (cumulative 150)
  • AEO-first content: category definition posts, benchmark data, entity disambiguation
  • Link velocity: 10+ DR 40+ backlinks earned through research content + podcast guesting
  • Category phrase ownership: "AI-native RevOps" surfaces Nina / DealARR in LLM answers

  • Quarterly State of RevOps report — content flywheel
  • Month 12: DealARR appears in 8-10 industry listicles, 10+ AEO citations, compounding MRR attribution from organic

Part 3 · Why an AI-native agency for an AI-native product

We build like you build — agentic stack, real-time data, human judgment in the loop

DealARR's positioning is "agentic AI for RevOps." The worst possible SEO partner for that product is an agency still using Google Docs, monthly PDF reports, and generic content writers. We're the opposite: a fully agentic SEO team. Same philosophy as your product, different domain.

Capability 01

Fine-tuned 27B SEO model

We run a custom Qwen3.5-27B model trained on 100,000+ citation-grounded chunks from Ahrefs, Moz, Backlinko, Search Engine Journal, and Google Search Central. Every strategic claim in this proposal traces back to a source — not "trust me" SEO. Nina, you'll see the citations in every deliverable.

Capability 02

MCPs piped to live data

Ahrefs, DataForSEO, GSC, GA4, Bing Webmaster — integrated via Model Context Protocol. Real-time SERP pulls, live keyword data, daily ranking deltas. Every weekly update is grounded in what the data said this week, not last month's cached PDF.

Capability 03

Brand-voice agents per client

Every client we work with gets bespoke writer, editor, validator, researcher, and schema agents trained on their brand. For DealARR, that means Nina's voice, the product's specificity, the competitive framing — scaled to 8-12 production pages/mo without dilution. No generic agency content dressed up as yours.

Capability 04

Anti-AI content validator

Every draft is scored across 8 dimensions + 200+ AI-detection patterns before it ships. Pages that read like generic AI output don't get published. Matters because (a) Google penalizes obvious AI content at scale and (b) LLMs decide who to cite partly on content quality signals — generic pages don't get chosen.

Capability 05

Automated weekly dashboards

Every Monday: 50 target keywords with rank deltas, AEO citation scorecard, listicle outreach status, signup attribution, top-10 competitor page changes. Two minutes to read. You know exactly what moved. Beats a monthly 40-slide SEO report that no one opens.

Capability 06

10-stage content pipeline

Brief → outline → draft → anti-AI check → SEO check → schema → internal links → publish → Week 2 impact check → Week 4 impact check. Each stage is a quality gate. This is how we ship 8-12 production-grade pages/mo instead of shipping 3-4 polished drafts plus a PDF roadmap.

The real lock-in

You could hire a traditional SEO agency at 2-3× our rate and get a senior strategist + three junior writers producing 4-6 pages/mo. You could hire a content freelancer at a third of our rate and get 8-10 generic AI-written pages/mo that Google will eventually de-rank. The agentic middle path — our positioning — ships 8-12 production-grade pages/mo with the strategic depth of the senior and the velocity of automation. That's the compound advantage over a 12-month engagement.
